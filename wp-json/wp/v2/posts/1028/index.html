The Physiology of Stress<\/h2>\n\n\n\n

To comprehend stress management, it’s crucial to first understand the biology of stress. Our bodies are equipped with a remarkable stress response system, often referred to as the “fight or flight” response. When we encounter a stressor, such as a tight deadline or a challenging situation, our brain’s hypothalamus signals the release of stress hormones, primarily cortisol and adrenaline.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

These hormones trigger a cascade of physiological changes. Heart rate and blood pressure increase, the respiratory rate quickens, and the body diverts energy away from non-essential functions, like digestion, to prepare for a perceived threat. In the short term, this response can be life-saving, but when stress becomes chronic, it can lead to a host of health issues, including anxiety, depression, heart disease, and more.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
